Recognizing PCOD & PCOS

Early recognition leads to better outcomes. Know the signs and use our self-checker tool.

Know the Signs

Common Symptoms

🔄

Irregular Menstrual Cycles

Infrequent, absent, or prolonged periods — one of the most common early signs of PCOS.

⚖️

Weight Gain / Difficulty Losing

Insulin resistance often makes weight management challenging despite diet efforts.

💇‍♀️

Excess Hair Growth (Hirsutism)

Unwanted facial and body hair due to elevated androgen levels.

🌟

Acne & Oily Skin

Hormonal imbalances trigger persistent acne, especially around jaw and chin.

💆‍♀️

Hair Thinning / Loss

Androgenic alopecia causing thinning at the crown and temples.

😴

Fatigue & Mood Changes

Chronic fatigue, anxiety, and depression are frequently linked to PCOS.

🍬

Sugar & Carb Cravings

Insulin resistance drives intense cravings and blood sugar fluctuations.

🤱

Fertility Challenges

Irregular ovulation is a leading cause of difficulty conceiving in women with PCOS.

🩸

Heavy or Painful Periods

When cycles occur, they may be heavier, more painful, or unusually long.

😔

Depression & Anxiety

Hormonal imbalances significantly impact mental health; mood disorders are common.

Know the Difference

PCOD vs PCOS

Aspect🌸 PCOD⚡ PCOS
SeverityMilder, more manageableComplex endocrine disorder
HormonesSlight hormonal imbalanceSignificant androgen excess
OvulationCan still ovulate regularlyOvulation disrupted or absent
FertilityConceiving is possibleFertility treatment often needed
PrevalenceMore common (~10% women)Less common (~5% women)
TreatmentDiet & lifestyle changesMedical + lifestyle intervention

Exercises for PCOS Warriors

Movement is medicine. These exercises help regulate hormones, manage weight, and boost mood.

🧘‍♀️

Supta Baddha Konasana

Reclined butterfly pose — opens hips, stimulates ovaries, and calms the nervous system. Ideal for hormonal balance.

Yoga15 min/dayBeginner
🙆‍♀️

Viparita Karani

Legs-up-the-wall pose reduces cortisol, improves circulation to pelvic organs, and relieves PCOS fatigue.

Yoga10 min/dayBeginner
🐍

Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

Stimulates the adrenal glands, stretches the abdominal organs, and helps regulate the menstrual cycle.

Yoga5 min/dayBeginner
🚶‍♀️

Brisk Walking

30 minutes daily improves insulin sensitivity by up to 20%. Low-impact, sustainable, and clinically proven for PCOS.

Cardio30 min/dayAll Levels
🚴‍♀️

Cycling / Stationary Bike

Low-impact cardio that burns fat without spiking cortisol — great for managing PCOS-related weight gain.

Cardio20–40 minIntermediate
🏊‍♀️

Swimming

Full-body low-impact exercise that improves cardiovascular health and reduces cortisol — perfect for PCOS management.

Cardio30 min/dayAll Levels
🏋️‍♀️

Resistance Training

Builds lean muscle mass which improves glucose uptake. 2–3 sessions/week significantly reduces androgen levels.

Pilates Core Work

Strengthens pelvic floor and core muscles, reduces insulin resistance, and improves body composition in PCOS.

Anulom-vilom and bhramari reduce cortisol and anxiety — key triggers for hormonal disruption in PCOS.
